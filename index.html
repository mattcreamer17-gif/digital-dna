//The way we would look at your stack.

Most of what each of your seven products does is not an AI problem. It is a traditional code and database problem (storing user plans, billing, content, session state). It is a rule-based problem (safety filters, language routing, kiosk flow control). Then a smaller 10% slice is the actual AI problem: the coach that adapts in real time, the historical figure that answers in character. Treating the whole stack as an AI product makes the inference bill larger than it needs to be. Deciding which layer each piece belongs on is the job, and it is the job Eduba does on engagements like this.

//The closest precedent on our side.

Feeld, the dating-and-relationships product company. CTO-led, small team, shipping AI into consumer features. A scoped sprint delivered a workshop, an organizational context architecture, and a strategic operations framework they still run off. The output was not a new feature. The output was the operating system for every feature they shipped after.

Method

Interpretable Context Methodology

The paper this connects to is Interpretable Context Methodology, published to ACM TiiS. It is the method for turning "DigitalDNA Core" from a brand line into a real folder-structure agent architecture you can point a museum procurement team or an enterprise legal team at.

For the governance questions that come with recreating historical figures and running health-adjacent AI coaching, we also publish the Ethics Engine (AuditEngine repo). A psychometric assessment tool for evaluating ideological and moral patterns in LLMs. Useful before a Museum of Democracy partner or a state AG asks how you audit what your characters say.
